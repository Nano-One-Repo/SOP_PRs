Standard Operating Procedure

Purchase Requisitions in Infor SyteLine

A step-by-step interactive guide for creating purchase requisitions — covering both Employee Self Service (ESS) and Full License workflows.

ESS License Limitations
What ESS users cannot do vs. Full License holders
The Employee Self Service (ESS) license is designed for basic self-service tasks. When it comes to purchase requisitions, ESS users will encounter several restrictions compared to Full License holders.
🔒
Simplified Form Access
ESS users only see the ESS portal view of the Purchase Requisition form. You will not have access to the full SyteLine rich client menus, navigation tree, or Quick Search (Ctrl+Q).
📉
Fewer Fields Available
Many advanced fields are hidden or read-only in ESS — including GL Account overrides, account distribution splits, project codes, buyer assignment, warehouse selection, and tax codes. These are typically set by your approver or purchasing agent.
👤
Own Requisitions Only
ESS users can only view and manage requisitions they have personally created. You cannot search, view, or edit requisitions submitted by other employees.
🚫
No PO Conversion
ESS users cannot convert approved requisitions into Purchase Orders. That step is handled by the Purchasing department using a Full License.
📊
No Reporting or Analytics
Purchasing reports, requisition-to-PO cross references, spend analysis, and other reporting forms are not available in the ESS portal.
⚙️
No Master Data Access
ESS users cannot view or modify master data such as the Vendor Master, Item Master, or Chart of Accounts. You can look up existing items/vendors within the requisition form, but cannot create or edit them.
✅
No Approval Authority
ESS users can submit requisitions for approval but cannot approve or reject requisitions from others. Approval authority requires a Full License with the appropriate workflow role.
📝
Limited Edit After Submission
Once a requisition is submitted, ESS users generally cannot recall or edit it. If changes are needed, contact your approver to reject it back to you, or reach out to the Purchasing team.
💡 If you need capabilities beyond the ESS license, speak with your System Administrator about a license upgrade. Your organization's Infor license agreement determines how many Full License seats are available.

Navigate to the Requisition Form
02
Open the ESS Purchase Requisition Form
Navigate through the Employee Self Service portal
ESS
Home > Employee Self Service > Purchase Requisitions
2a From the SyteLine homepage, click on the Employee Self Service tile or menu item. This is your main ESS dashboard.
2b Locate and click Purchase Requisitions in the ESS menu. Depending on your configuration, this may be under a "Purchasing" or "Requests" sub-menu.
2c The Purchase Requisition List will open, showing any existing requisitions you've previously created. Click New to create a new requisition.
💡 ESS navigation is simplified compared to full-client. If you don't see "Purchase Requisitions," your administrator may need to enable it in your ESS configuration or role.
02
Open the Purchase Requisition Form
Navigate through the SyteLine client menus
Full
Menu > Purchasing > Purchase Requisitions > Purchase Requisitions
2a From the SyteLine main menu or navigation pane, expand the Purchasing module.
2b Under Purchasing, expand Purchase Requisitions and click Purchase Requisitions to open the form.
2c Alternatively, use the Quick Search bar (Ctrl+Q) and type "Purchase Requisitions" to jump directly to the form.
2d The form will open in Browse mode showing existing records. Click the New button (or press Ctrl+N) on the toolbar to create a new requisition.
⌨️ Full license users can also add the Purchase Requisitions form to their Favorites for quicker access in the future.
Complete the Requisition Header
03
Fill In the Requisition Header
Set top-level details: requester, date, status
Both

The header captures who is requesting, when it's needed, and general categorization. Some fields auto-populate based on your login.

3a Requisition Number — typically auto-generated by the system. Do not modify unless your organization uses manual numbering.
3b Requester — will default to your user/employee ID. Verify this is correct.
3c Requisition Date — defaults to today's date. Adjust only if backdating is permitted by policy.
3d Needed-By Date — enter the date you need the goods/services. This drives purchasing urgency and planning.
3e Status — will show as "Open" or "New" for a new record. Do not change this manually.
Full License — Additional Fields Full license users may also see: Warehouse (default receiving warehouse), Ship To address, Buyer Code, and Priority. Fill these if applicable to your organization's workflow.
⚠️ Do not change the Status field manually. Status is managed by the system through the approval workflow.
Add Line Items
04
Add Requisition Line Items
Enter item details, quantities, and costs
Both

Each line item represents a distinct product or service you're requesting. You can add multiple lines to a single requisition.

4a Click Add Line or navigate to the Lines tab/grid at the bottom of the form.
4b Item Number — If ordering a stocked/cataloged item, enter or look up the item number. The description, U/M, and cost may auto-populate from the item master. For non-stock items, leave blank and enter a manual description.
4c Description — Provide a clear, specific description of what you're ordering. Be detailed enough for the buyer to source correctly (e.g., "Safety goggles, ANSI Z87.1 rated, clear lens" rather than "goggles").
4d Quantity — Enter the number of units needed.
4e Unit of Measure (U/M) — Select from the dropdown (EA, BX, CS, etc.). Ensure this matches how the vendor prices the item.
4f Unit Cost / Estimated Price — Enter the estimated cost per unit. If unknown, provide your best estimate — this helps the approval process.
4g Vendor — If you have a preferred vendor, enter or look up their vendor number. This is optional on the requisition but helps purchasing.
4h To add more lines, click Add Line again and repeat the above for each item/service.
Full License — Additional Line Fields Full license users may also see and complete: GL Account (the expense account to charge), Project Code, Cost Center / Department, Delivery Address, and Tax Code. Fill these according to your organization's chart of accounts and purchasing policy.
ESS License — Simplified View ESS users typically see fewer fields per line. The GL Account and other advanced fields may be auto-assigned based on your department or the approver may set them during review. Focus on providing accurate item, quantity, and cost information.
💡 Use the Item Lookup (magnifying glass icon) to search existing items in the system. This ensures correct item numbers and avoids duplicates.
Account Assignment
05
Assign GL Accounts & Cost Centers
Map each line to the correct financial account
Full

Full license users are responsible for assigning the correct General Ledger (GL) accounts to each requisition line. This controls which department or project budget is charged.

5a On each line item, locate the Account or GL Account field. Click the lookup to browse the Chart of Accounts.
5b Select the appropriate expense account for the purchase (e.g., 5000-100 for Office Supplies, 5200-200 for Maintenance Parts).
5c If your organization uses account dimensions (department, project, cost center), fill in each segment as required.
5d If splitting the cost across multiple accounts, use the Account Distribution feature (if available in your configuration) to allocate percentages.
⚠️ Incorrect GL coding is one of the top reasons requisitions are rejected during approval. Double-check with your finance team if unsure which account to use.
05
Verify Auto-Assigned Accounts (ESS)
Confirm default account coding
ESS

For ESS users, the GL account and cost center are typically auto-assigned based on your department or employee profile setup in the system.

5a Review the Account field if it is visible. It should display your department's default expense account.
5b If the account looks incorrect or you're purchasing for a different department/project, note this in the Notes/Comments field so the approver can reassign it.
✅ In most ESS configurations, your approver or purchasing agent will finalize GL coding during the approval step. Focus on providing accurate descriptions and costs.
Notes & Attachments
06
Add Notes, Comments & Attachments
Provide supporting justification and documents
Both

Supporting documentation speeds up the approval process and gives the buyer context for sourcing decisions.

6a Notes / Comments — Enter the business justification for the purchase. Example: "Replacement parts for CNC Machine #4 — current bearings are worn and causing quality defects per NCR-2026-0412."
6b Attachments — Click the attachment/paperclip icon to upload supporting files such as vendor quotes, spec sheets, or photos.
6c Ensure files are in common formats (PDF, XLSX, DOCX, JPG/PNG) and are under your system's file size limit.
Full License Full license users can also use the Internal Notes vs. External Notes distinction — internal notes stay within your organization, while external notes may carry forward to the Purchase Order sent to the vendor.
📎 Always attach vendor quotes for purchases above your organization's threshold (e.g., $500+). This significantly speeds up approval.
Review & Submit
07
Review the Requisition
Verify all details before submitting
Both

Before submitting, perform a final review of every field and line item. Errors at this stage cause delays in the approval and purchasing process.

7a Header Check — Confirm requester name, needed-by date, and any header-level fields are correct.
7b Line Items Check — Review each line for: correct item/description, accurate quantity, correct U/M, reasonable unit cost, and (if applicable) correct GL account.
7c Total Amount — Review the requisition total. Ensure it aligns with your budget expectations and approval thresholds.
7d Attachments — Confirm all supporting documents have been uploaded and are legible.
⚠️ Common rejection reasons: missing description detail, wrong GL account, no vendor quote attached for high-value items, unrealistic needed-by dates.
08
Save & Submit for Approval
Finalize the requisition and route it to your approver
Both
8a Click Save (Ctrl+S) to save the requisition. This creates the record without submitting it — you can still make edits.
8b Once satisfied, click Submit (or change the status to "Submitted" depending on your configuration). This routes the requisition into the approval workflow.
8c Note the Requisition Number for your records. You'll use this to track the status of your request.
ESS License In the ESS portal, the Submit button is typically a prominent action at the top of the form. After submission, the requisition is routed to your manager or designated approver based on the workflow rules configured in the system.
Full License Full license users may have a Workflow toolbar button that shows the approval chain. You can also see which approver the requisition will be routed to before submitting. Some configurations allow selecting from multiple approval paths.
🚀 After submission, your approver will receive a notification (email or SyteLine alert). You'll be notified when it's approved or if changes are requested.
Post-Submission
09
Track Requisition Status
Monitor approval progress and respond to requests
Both

After submission, monitor your requisition through the approval and purchasing lifecycle.

StatusMeaning
Open / DraftSaved but not yet submitted — still editable
SubmittedSent to the approval workflow — awaiting approver action
ApprovedApproved by all required approvers — ready for purchasing to convert to a PO
RejectedReturned by an approver — review comments and resubmit with corrections
Converted / ClosedA Purchase Order has been generated from this requisition
9a Return to the Purchase Requisition list to check the Status column for your requisition number.
9b If Rejected: open the requisition, review the approver's comments, make the requested changes, and re-submit.
9c If Approved: purchasing will create a PO. You can track the PO number once generated — it will typically appear linked on the requisition record.
Full License — Advanced Tracking Full license users can also use the Requisition-to-PO Cross Reference form, and run Purchasing reports to track all requisitions by status, requester, date range, and more.
🔔 Set up Infor notifications (email or Ming.le alerts) for requisition status changes so you're automatically informed when your requisition is approved, rejected, or converted to a PO.
